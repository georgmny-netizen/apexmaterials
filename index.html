Quality & Supply Control

Qualification-oriented supply signals for controlled procurement environments.

Quality is defined by specification compliance, verified by defined analytical methods, and maintained through controlled supply processes.

Quality Management

  • Operations are conducted within a controlled quality management framework with defined responsibility and documentation control.
  • Material sourcing, verification, and delivery are aligned with defined requirements
  • Verification methods are selected according to specification and control requirement
  • Supply scope is limited to specification-controlled materials and defined delivery conditions

Documentation Package

  • Documentation includes certificate of analysis, lot traceability, and measurement method reference.
  • Each delivery can be linked to specification, analytical data, and documentation set
  • Documentation package is aligned with quoted scope and approval requirements
  • Representative documentation set is available upon request
  • Unspecified requirements are not assumed and may require clarification prior to supply

Non-Conformance & Control

  • Non-conformance is handled through documented review and corrective action process
  • Non-conforming material is identified and not released without review
  • Material changes affecting specification or supply conditions are subject to prior communication and agreement
  • Supply control and documentation are managed under defined internal quality responsibility
  • Material suitability remains the responsibility of the requesting party unless explicitly defined in specification

Continuity & Delivery

  • Supply is structured to support repeat orders and program continuity under consistent specification control.
  • Designed for qualification-critical and repeat supply programs
  • Material origin and export classification can be disclosed upon request, subject to supply chain constraints and applicable regulations
  • Delivery terms are defined per quotation and may include international commercial terms (Incoterms)
